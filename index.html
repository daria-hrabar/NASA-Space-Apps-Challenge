About ASTER True Color Imagery

What is ASTER? The Advanced Spaceborne Thermal Emission and Reflection Radiometer (ASTER) captures high-resolution images in visible and infrared wavelengths, providing detailed views of Earth's surface.

How to interpret the imagery:

  • Green areas: Indicate healthy, dense vegetation.
  • Brownish areas: Suggest degraded vegetation or sparse plant cover.
  • Gray/white areas: Represent bare soil, urban areas, or deforested land.

Why it matters: ASTER imagery helps scientists monitor land use changes, assess environmental impacts, and track deforestation over time.

About NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)

What is NDVI? NDVI measures the health and density of vegetation by analyzing the difference between near-infrared and red light reflected by plants.

How to read the values:

  • 0.5 - 1.0: Dense, healthy vegetation (forests, crops)
  • 0.2 - 0.5: Sparse vegetation (grasslands, degraded areas)
  • 0.0 - 0.2: Bare soil, water, or urban areas

Why it matters: NDVI helps scientists track deforestation, monitor crop health, and assess environmental changes over time.

üåç Global Climate Impact

The Amazon rainforest is often called the "lungs of the Earth" because it produces 20% of the world's oxygen. Your investigation revealed that human activities are destroying this critical ecosystem at an alarming rate.

  • Carbon Storage Loss: The Amazon stores 80-120 billion tons of carbon - equivalent to 10 years of global emissions
  • Climate Feedback: Deforestation creates a dangerous cycle where less forest means less rainfall, leading to more fires and further deforestation
  • Biodiversity Crisis: The Amazon is home to 10% of all known species on Earth, many now threatened with extinction
